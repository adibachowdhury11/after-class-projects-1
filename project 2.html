History of My School:

After the British government in India replaced Persian with English as the official language, Indian Muslims abandoned English. Consequently, Indian Muslims soon faced ruin due to a lack of education. Sir Syed Ahmad Khan took steps to protect Indian Muslims from destruction. After much struggle, he encouraged them to learn English and established the Aligarh University. This history is well-known.

The Muslims of Brahmanbaria also faced the risk of ruin due to lack of education. Following Sir Syed Ahmad Khan's example, several prominent Muslim figures and social workers in Brahmanbaria city came forward to establish an English school in the city. They were: the late Munshi Abdul Kadir, the late Abbas Khan, the late Syed Mohammad Ali, the late Khan Bahadur Abdur Rauf, and the late Khan Sahib Ansar Ali Muktar.

On March 3, 1910, they established George Minor School (George M. E. School) on a vacant plot of land on the eastern side of Brahmanbaria Sadar Thana. It is noteworthy that during British rule, a high school could not be established directly. Initially, it was a minor school and later upgraded to a high school. Due to a significant increase in students, the school was relocated to the Modail region (its current location). In 1914, it was upgraded to George High English School and in 1919, it received permanent recognition from the University of Calcutta. Soon, the school became filled with both Hindu and Muslim students due to its high-quality teaching staff.
